SLEEP HEALTH

Eastern Sleep Wisdom

Discover the ancient Chinese approach to sleep. Traditional Chinese Medicine views sleep as essential for health, governed by the natural flow of Yin and Yang energy throughout the day. Learn how to align your sleep with nature's rhythms for deeper, more restorative rest.

ANCIENT WISDOM

The Chinese View of Sleep

In Traditional Chinese Medicine, sleep is governed by the balance of Yin (restful, cooling energy) and Yang (active, warming energy). When Yang energy retreats inward at night, Yin energy rises, allowing deep sleep. This natural cycle is disrupted by modern lifestyle factors like artificial light, stress, and irregular schedules.

The Chinese also believe that sleep quality reflects the health of specific organ systems. Different times of night are associated with different organs, which is why certain sleep disturbances may indicate specific health concerns.

BODY CLOCK

The Traditional Chinese Organ Clock

According to TCM, different organs are most active during specific two-hour windows throughout the day.

🌙

11 PM - 1 AM: Gallbladder

The body begins its deepest restoration. Gallbladder detoxifies and processes decisions from the day. Deep sleep is essential during this time.

💤

1 AM - 3 AM: Liver

Liver detoxifies blood and stores energy for the next day. Waking during this time may indicate liver stagnation or excess heat.

🫁

3 AM - 5 AM: Lungs

Lungs distribute oxygen and Qi throughout the body. This is when deep sleep transitions to lighter sleep. Early morning waking may relate to lung or grief issues.

☀️

5 AM - 7 AM: Large Intestine

The body prepares to wake. This is the ideal time for bowel movements and morning routines. Natural light exposure helps reset circadian rhythm.

SLEEP CALCULATOR

Optimize Your Sleep Cycles

Each sleep cycle lasts approximately 90 minutes. Waking at the end of a cycle helps you feel refreshed and alert.

EVENING RITUAL

Traditional Chinese Bedtime Routine

Follow this ancient-inspired evening routine for optimal sleep quality.

⏰

2 Hours Before Bed

Wind Down: Dim lights, avoid screens, and engage in calming activities like reading or gentle stretching. Prepare your mind for rest.

🦶

1 Hour Before Bed

Foot Soak: Soak feet in warm water with ginger or Epsom salts for 15-20 minutes. This draws energy downward and promotes relaxation.

💆

30 Minutes Before Bed

Self-Massage: Massage the soles of your feet (Kidney 1 point) and the space between your eyebrows (Third Eye point) to calm the mind.

🫖

Bedtime Tea

Soothing Brew: Sip warm chamomile, chrysanthemum, or jujube date tea. Avoid caffeine after 2 PM and heavy meals close to bedtime.

SLEEP POSITIONS

Optimal Sleep Positions

Traditional Chinese Medicine recommends specific sleeping positions for better health.

😴

Right Side Sleeping

The Traditional Recommendation

TCM recommends sleeping on your right side, as this position supports the natural curve of the stomach and promotes better digestion. It also reduces heartburn and improves circulation.

Benefits

  • Improves digestion
  • Reduces acid reflux
  • Supports heart function
🛌

Back Sleeping with Support

For Spinal Alignment

Sleeping on your back with a small pillow under the knees helps maintain the natural curve of the spine. Add a pillow under the knees for lower back support.

Benefits

  • Aligns spine properly
  • Reduces pressure points
  • Promotes even breathing
🌙

The Fetal Position

Curled and Comfortable

Curled on your side with knees toward chest, this position can be comforting. However, avoid curling too tightly as it can restrict deep breathing.

Benefits

  • Feels safe and secure
  • Good for lower back
  • Reduces snoring

Eastern Sleep Wisdom

Time-tested principles for better sleep from Traditional Chinese Medicine.

  • ✓ Go to bed before 11 PM when Gallbladder energy peaks
  • ✓ Keep your bedroom cool, dark, and quiet
  • ✓ Avoid stimulating activities and bright screens 2 hours before bed
  • ✓ Eat your last meal at least 3 hours before sleeping
  • ✓ Practice deep breathing or meditation before sleep
  • ✓ Sleep with your head pointing north (magnetic alignment)
  • ✓ Wake with the sunrise when possible to sync with natural rhythms
