Sunday - Restorative Day

📅 June 21, 2026 • Week of June 15–21 • Focus: Rest & Recovery
Advertisement Space (728x90)

Sunday is Restorative Day, dedicated to rest and recovery. After a week of activity, the body needs gentle, easy-to-digest foods that restore energy without burdening the digestive system. Light, warm, and simple meals allow the body to focus on repair and rejuvenation.

Main Meal Plan

🌅 Breakfast: Light Fruit and Yogurt with Honey

Ingredients: Plain yogurt, seasonal fruit (pears, figs, or berries), 1 tbsp honey, 1 tbsp pumpkin seeds

Method: Spoon yogurt into a bowl. Top with sliced fruit, a drizzle of honey, and pumpkin seeds. Eat slowly and mindfully.

TCM Notes: Yogurt is cooling and moistening, perfect for restoration. Pears moisten the lungs and counter dryness. Honey harmonizes all ingredients. This light breakfast gives the digestive system a rest.

🥣 Lunch: Gentle Vegetable Broth with Soft Grains

Ingredients: Vegetable broth, ½ cup soft-cooked millet or quinoa, zucchini, carrots, spinach, a few goji berries

Method: Cook grains until very soft. Heat broth, add diced vegetables, simmer until tender. Add grains and goji berries. Serve warm.

TCM Notes: Millet is neutral and easy to digest, ideal for restoration. Soft vegetables provide nutrients without strain. Goji berries nourish the liver and kidneys, supporting the body's recovery processes.

🍚 Dinner: Simple Steamed Vegetables with Rice

Ingredients: Jasmine rice, steamed broccoli, steamed carrots, a drizzle of sesame oil, pinch of salt

Method: Cook rice. Steam vegetables until tender. Arrange simply on a plate, drizzle with sesame oil, and season lightly. The simplicity is intentional.

TCM Notes: Simple food is healing food. Steaming preserves nutrients while keeping meals light. Jasmine rice is sweet and neutral, supporting the spleen without burden. This meal prepares the body for restful sleep.

Alternative Meal Plan

🔄 Alternative Breakfast: Chrysanthemum Tea with Rice Crackers

Steep chrysanthemum flowers in hot water for 5 minutes. Drink the tea while nibbling on plain rice crackers with a thin spread of sweet red bean paste. Chrysanthemum clears heat and calms the spirit—perfect for a restful Sunday morning.

🔄 Alternative Lunch: Congee with Chicken and Ginger

Classic chicken congee—rice cooked down with shredded chicken, ginger, and a pinch of salt until creamy. Add a soft-boiled egg and sliced scallions. Congee is the ultimate restorative food, requiring almost no digestive effort.

🔄 Alternative Dinner: Miso Soup with Tofu and Seaweed

Simple miso soup with cubes of silken tofu and a handful of wakame seaweed. Ready in 10 minutes. This is perhaps the lightest, most restorative dinner possible—warming, nourishing, and incredibly easy to digest.

"Rest is not idleness. To lie on the grass on a Sunday afternoon, listening to the murmur of the stream, is hardly a waste of time." — John Lubbock (aligned with TCM's Sunday restoration philosophy)
