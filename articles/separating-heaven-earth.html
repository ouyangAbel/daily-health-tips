Separating Heaven and Earth

📅 June 16, 2026 • Baduanjin Movement 3
Advertisement Space (728x90)

Chinese Name: 调理脾胃须单举 (Tiáo Lǐ Pí Wèi Xū Dān Jǔ)

Meaning: "Regulating the Spleen and Stomach by Raising One Hand Alone"

This gentle twisting movement specifically targets the digestive system. By stretching one hand upward and the other downward, you create a subtle wringing motion in the torso that massages the spleen and stomach organs. It's particularly beneficial for anyone experiencing digestive discomfort, bloating, or low energy after meals.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Starting Position

Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, knees slightly bent. Hold both hands in front of your abdomen, palms facing each other as if holding a ball.

Step 2: Separate Hands

Inhale and slowly raise your left hand upward, palm facing the sky. At the same time, press your right hand downward, palm facing the earth. Your arms should form a vertical line—left hand reaching up, right hand pressing down.

Step 3: Stretch and Twist

At full extension, feel the stretch along the left side of your torso. Your spine should lengthen, not twist aggressively. Hold for 3-5 seconds while breathing deeply. The stretch should feel like a gentle wringing of the midsection.

Step 4: Return to Center

Exhale and slowly bring both hands back to the starting position in front of your abdomen. Relax for a moment.

Step 5: Switch Sides

Repeat on the other side: raise your right hand up while pressing your left hand down. Alternate sides for 6-8 repetitions total (3-4 per side).

Key Points

  • Keep your hips facing forward—the stretch comes from the torso, not hip rotation
  • Press firmly through the heel of the upward hand and the palm of the downward hand
  • Keep your shoulders relaxed even during the stretch
  • Breathe naturally; don't hold your breath during the stretch
  • The movement should feel like wringing a towel—gentle but effective

Health Benefits

  • Improves digestion: The gentle twisting massages the stomach and intestines, promoting peristalsis
  • Stimulates the spleen and stomach: In TCM, these organs govern nutrient absorption and energy production
  • Enhances flexibility: The side stretch improves lateral flexibility of the spine
  • Relieves bloating: The wringing motion helps move trapped gas through the digestive tract
  • Boosts energy: Better digestion means more efficient nutrient absorption and sustained energy
"When heaven and earth separate, the middle realm finds its balance. The spleen rises, the stomach descends, and digestion flows like a mountain stream." — Traditional Baduanjin instruction
