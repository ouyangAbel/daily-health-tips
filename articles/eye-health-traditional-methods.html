Eye Health: Traditional Chinese Methods to Protect Your Vision

📅 June 17, 2026 • Daily Wellness
Advertisement Space (728x90)

In today's digital world, we spend hours staring at screens—computers, phones, tablets—leading to eye strain, dryness, fatigue, and even worsening vision. Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM) has addressed eye health for thousands of years, offering natural techniques to protect and rejuvenate your eyes.

According to TCM, the eyes are connected to the Liver meridian and depend on the smooth flow of Qi and Blood to stay healthy. When Liver Blood is deficient or Qi stagnates, eye problems arise. Here are proven traditional Chinese methods to keep your eyes healthy.

1. Palming: The Instant Eye Relaxer

This simple technique relaxes the eye muscles and relieves strain in just minutes.

  • Rub your palms together vigorously for 10-15 seconds until warm
  • Cup your warm palms over your closed eyes (don't press on the eyeballs)
  • Breathe deeply and relax for 2-3 minutes
  • The warmth and darkness allow your eye muscles to fully relax

When to use: Every 30-60 minutes during screen time, or whenever your eyes feel tired.

2. Acupressure Points for Eye Health

🎯 Jingming (睛明) — Bladder 1

Location: In the inner corner of the eye, where the upper and lower eyelids meet, slightly above the inner canthus.

Technique: Close your eyes gently. Use your index finger to press softly on this point. Hold for 30 seconds while breathing deeply. Do not press too hard.

Benefits: Relieves eye fatigue, improves vision, treats dry eyes, and helps with blurry vision.

🎯 Zanzhu (攒竹) — Bladder 2

Location: At the inner end of the eyebrow, in the small indentation at the inner brow bone.

Technique: Use your thumbs to press both points simultaneously. Apply gentle upward pressure for 1-2 minutes.

Benefits: Relieves frontal headaches, eye strain, twitching eyelids, and sinus pressure around the eyes.

🎯 Si Zhong Kong (丝竹空) — Triple Burner 23

Location: At the outer end of the eyebrow, in the small depression at the outer brow bone.

Technique: Use your index fingers to press both temples gently. Hold for 1-2 minutes with light circular motions.

Benefits: Relieves eye pressure, temporal headaches, and blurry vision. Excellent for eye fatigue from screen use.

🎯 Chengqi (承泣) — Stomach 1

Location: Directly below the pupil, in the depression at the lower edge of the eye socket (orbital ridge).

Technique: Close your eyes. Use your index fingers to press gently upward against the bone. Hold for 1 minute. Be very gentle—do not press on the eyeball.

Benefits: Improves blood circulation to the eyes, relieves eye fatigue, treats twitching, and helps with watery eyes.

3. Chinese Eye Exercises (眼保健操)

Eye exercises have been practiced in Chinese schools for decades to prevent myopia. This 5-minute routine can be done anywhere:

🔄 Complete Eye Exercise Routine

  • Step 1 — Rubbing Jingming: Press and rub the inner eye corners 8 times
  • Step 2 — Pressing Zanzhu: Press the inner brow points 8 times
  • Step 3 — Pressing Sibai: Press the points below the pupils 8 times
  • Step 4 — Circling Temples: Massage both temples in circles 8 times each direction
  • Step 5 — Eye Rolling: Slowly roll your eyes in large circles, 8 times clockwise, 8 times counterclockwise
  • Step 6 — Palming: Finish with 1 minute of palming to relax

4. Tea Therapy for Eye Health

TCM recommends specific teas to nourish the Liver and benefit the eyes:

  • Chrysanthemum Tea (菊花茶): The most famous eye-nourishing tea. Clears Liver heat, brightens the eyes, and relieves dryness. Drink 1-2 cups daily.
  • Goji Berry Tea (枸杞茶): Nourishes Liver Blood and Kidney Yin. Rich in zeaxanthin, which protects the retina. Steep 10-15 berries in hot water for 5 minutes.
  • Chrysanthemum + Goji Combination: The classic pairing for maximum eye benefits. Add both to hot water and drink daily.

5. The 20-20-20 Rule (adapted from TCM)

While this modern guideline aligns with TCM principles, it's highly effective:

  • Every 20 minutes — look away from your screen
  • Focus on something 20 feet away — at least 6 meters distant
  • For at least 20 seconds — allow your eye muscles to relax
  • Add: Blink consciously 10 times to moisten your eyes

6. Foods for Eye Health (TCM Dietary Therapy)

In TCM, "food is medicine." These foods nourish the Liver and benefit the eyes:

  • Carrots: Rich in beta-carotene, nourish Liver Blood
  • Blueberries: Contain anthocyanins that protect retinal blood vessels
  • Spinach: Rich in lutein and zeaxanthin, protects against macular degeneration
  • Eggs: Yolks contain lutein and zinc for eye protection
  • Fatty fish: Omega-3s reduce dry eye symptoms
  • Black sesame seeds: TCM classic for nourishing Kidney Yin and brightening eyes

💡 Daily Eye Care Tips

  • Adjust screen brightness to match your surrounding light
  • Keep screen 20-26 inches from your eyes
  • Use artificial tears if your eyes feel dry
  • Sleep 7-8 hours — eyes repair during deep sleep
  • Wear sunglasses outdoors to protect from UV damage
  • Practice eye exercises at least once daily

When to See a Doctor

These techniques are for prevention and mild symptoms. Consult an eye specialist if you experience:

  • Sudden vision loss or blurred vision
  • Severe eye pain or redness
  • Flashing lights or floating spots
  • Persistent eye discharge or swelling
  • Vision problems that don't improve with rest
"The eyes are the windows to the Liver. When we care for our eyes, we care for our body's entire system of renewal." — Traditional Chinese Medicine principle
