Drawing the Bow

📅 June 16, 2026 • Baduanjin Movement 2
Advertisement Space (728x90)

Chinese Name: 左右开弓似射雕 (Zuǒ Yòu Kāi Gōng Sì Shè Diāo)

Meaning: "Drawing the Bow to the Left and Right Like Shooting a Hawk"

This movement mimics an archer drawing a bow, combining a deep horse stance with expansive arm movements. It is one of the most physically engaging Baduanjin exercises, building leg strength while opening the chest and stimulating the lung and kidney meridians.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Horse Stance

Step your feet apart to about twice shoulder-width. Bend your knees and sink your hips as if sitting on an invisible chair. Keep your back straight and your weight centered. This is the horse stance (马步), the foundation of the movement.

Step 2: Prepare the Bow

Cross your wrists in front of your chest, palms facing inward. Your left hand should be on the outside if you're drawing to the left first.

Step 3: Draw the Bow

Extend your left arm to the left side, forming a "V" shape with your thumb and index finger (like an archer's hand). Simultaneously, pull your right hand back to your right shoulder as if drawing a bowstring. Turn your head to look along your extended left arm. Breathe in deeply.

Step 4: Hold and Breathe

Hold the position for 3-5 seconds. Feel the expansion across your chest and the engagement in your legs. Breathe deeply into the stretch.

Step 5: Release and Switch

Slowly bring your hands back to the center, crossing at the wrists again. Repeat on the right side: extend your right arm and pull back with your left hand. Alternate sides for 6-8 repetitions total (3-4 per side).

Key Points

  • Keep your horse stance stable—don't bob up and down between sides
  • The extended arm should be at shoulder height, not higher
  • Spread your fingers wide on the "bow" hand to activate the lung meridian
  • Keep your torso upright—don't lean to the side
  • Breathe deeply into the chest during the hold

Health Benefits

  • Strengthens legs and core: The horse stance builds isometric strength in the quadriceps, glutes, and core
  • Improves lung capacity: The chest expansion deepens breathing and strengthens respiratory muscles
  • Enhances focus: The coordinated movement of eyes, arms, and legs trains concentration
  • Stimulates meridians: The lung and kidney meridians are activated through the arm extension and deep breathing
  • Improves posture: Strengthening the upper back muscles counteracts rounded shoulders
"Like an archer aiming at the distant hawk, the mind focuses, the body steadies, and the breath flows like a river. In this stillness, strength is born." — Traditional Baduanjin instruction
