Thursday - Energizing Day

📅 June 18, 2026 • Week of June 15–21 • Focus: Heart & Small Intestine
Advertisement Space (728x90)

Thursday is Energizing Day, focusing on the heart and small intestine. The heart is the emperor of all organs in TCM, housing the spirit (Shen). Red foods, warming spices, and meals that bring joy all support heart health. The small intestine pairs with the heart and governs the separation of clear from turbid.

Main Meal Plan

🌅 Breakfast: Oatmeal with Berries and Honey

Ingredients: 1 cup rolled oats, mixed berries (blueberries, strawberries), 1 tbsp honey, 1 tbsp chia seeds, almond milk

Method: Cook oats with almond milk until creamy. Top with fresh berries, a drizzle of honey, and chia seeds.

TCM Notes: Berries are red and purple, nourishing heart blood. Honey is sweet and harmonizing. Oatmeal provides sustained energy without spiking blood sugar, supporting the heart's need for steady fuel.

🌯 Lunch: Whole Grain Wrap with Grilled Vegetables

Ingredients: Whole grain tortilla, grilled bell peppers (red), zucchini, eggplant, hummus, fresh herbs

Method: Grill sliced vegetables until charred and tender. Spread hummus on tortilla, layer vegetables, add fresh herbs, and wrap tightly.

TCM Notes: Red bell peppers are one of the best heart-nourishing vegetables. Grilling adds warmth. The whole grain provides sustained energy for the afternoon.

🍅 Dinner: Tomato-Based Soup with Legumes

Ingredients: 4 large tomatoes, 1 cup cooked chickpeas or lentils, onion, garlic, basil, vegetable broth, olive oil

Method: Sauté onion and garlic, add chopped tomatoes and broth, simmer 20 minutes. Add legumes and cook 10 more minutes. Blend partially for a chunky texture. Finish with fresh basil.

TCM Notes: Tomatoes are red, nourishing the heart. Legumes provide plant protein and are sweet, supporting the spleen-heart axis. This warming soup supports Yang energy.

Alternative Meal Plan

🔄 Alternative Breakfast: Red Date and Goji Tea with Toast

Steep 5 red dates (jujubes) and a handful of goji berries in hot water for 10 minutes. Drink the tea and eat the softened dates. Serve with whole grain toast and almond butter. Red dates are the premier heart-nourishing food in TCM.

🔄 Alternative Lunch: Beet and Quinoa Power Salad

Roasted beets, cooked quinoa, walnuts, arugula, and a balsamic vinaigrette. Beets are deeply nourishing to heart blood and support circulation. Walnuts add omega-3s for heart health.

🔄 Alternative Dinner: Watermelon and Feta Salad with Mint

Cubed watermelon, crumbled feta, fresh mint, and a light olive oil drizzle. Watermelon is cooling and clears heart heat in summer. Mint adds a refreshing touch. Perfect for warm evenings.

"The heart is the ruler of all organs. When the heart is at peace, all organs are at peace." — Huangdi Neijing (Yellow Emperor's Classic of Internal Medicine)
