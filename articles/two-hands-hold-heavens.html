Two Hands Hold the Heavens

📅 June 16, 2026 • Baduanjin Movement 1
Advertisement Space (728x90)

Chinese Name: 双手托天理三焦 (Shuāng Shǒu Tuō Tiān Lǐ Sān Jiāo)

Meaning: "Two Hands Hold Up the Heavens to Regulate the Triple Burner"

This is the opening movement of the Baduanjin sequence and arguably the most important. It stretches the entire body from fingertips to toes, creating space in the torso and stimulating the Triple Burner (三焦)—the TCM organ system that governs the coordination of all internal organs.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Starting Position

Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, toes pointing forward. Relax your shoulders and let your arms hang naturally at your sides. Breathe deeply for 3 breaths to settle your mind.

Step 2: Interlace Fingers

Bring your hands in front of your lower abdomen. Interlace your fingers with palms facing upward. Keep your elbows slightly bent and your shoulders relaxed.

Step 3: Press Upward

Slowly raise your hands up the center line of your body. When they reach chest height, flip your palms to face the sky. Continue pressing upward until your arms are fully extended above your head. Look up at your hands.

Step 4: Stretch and Hold

At the top of the movement, stretch your entire body upward as if trying to touch the sky. Hold for 3-5 seconds while breathing deeply. Feel the stretch from your fingers down through your torso to your feet.

Step 5: Release

Slowly lower your arms out to the sides in a wide arc, palms facing down. Exhale as you lower your arms back to the starting position. Relax your shoulders completely.

Repeat: 6-8 times. Coordinate movement with breath—inhale as you press up, exhale as you lower.

Key Points

  • Keep your shoulders down and relaxed even as you press up—don't hunch
  • Your spine should elongate, not arch backward
  • Press through the heels of your palms, not your fingers
  • Keep your gaze soft—look up without straining your neck
  • Move slowly and smoothly; this is not a race

Health Benefits

  • Improves spinal alignment: The upward stretch decompresses the vertebrae and improves posture
  • Opens chest and shoulders: Counteracts the hunched posture from desk work and phone use
  • Regulates the Triple Burner: In TCM, this movement harmonizes the upper, middle, and lower body organ systems
  • Stretches the fascia: The continuous stretch from fingers to toes releases tension throughout the body
  • Improves breathing: Opening the chest allows deeper, fuller breaths

Common Mistakes

  • Raising the shoulders toward the ears—keep them dropped
  • Arching the lower back—keep the core gently engaged
  • Moving too fast—slow movement builds awareness
  • Holding the breath—breathe naturally throughout
"When the hands press upward, the Triple Burner opens like a gate. Qi flows freely through the three regions, and the organs find their harmony." — Traditional Baduanjin instruction
