Friday - Harmonizing Day

📅 June 19, 2026 • Week of June 15–21 • Focus: Balanced Nutrition
Advertisement Space (728x90)

Friday is Harmonizing Day, emphasizing balanced all-round nutrition. As the work week ends, we focus on meals that include all five flavors (sweet, sour, bitter, salty, pungent) and support overall harmony. This is a day for colorful, varied meals that nourish all organ systems equally.

Main Meal Plan

🌅 Breakfast: Multigrain Toast with Avocado and Eggs

Ingredients: 2 slices multigrain bread, 1 avocado, 2 eggs, cherry tomatoes, sesame seeds, chili flakes

Method: Toast bread. Mash avocado and spread on toast. Top with poached or fried eggs. Add halved cherry tomatoes, sesame seeds, and chili flakes.

TCM Notes: This breakfast includes all five flavors: sweet (bread), sour (tomatoes), bitter (avocado), salty (sesame), pungent (chili). Eggs provide balanced protein that supports all organs.

🍚 Lunch: Balanced Bowl with Grains, Protein, and Vegetables

Ingredients: Brown rice or quinoa, grilled chicken or tofu, roasted sweet potato, steamed broccoli, pickled vegetables, tahini dressing

Method: Arrange grains in a bowl. Top with protein, roasted sweet potato, and steamed broccoli. Add a side of pickled vegetables. Drizzle with tahini dressing.

TCM Notes: The balanced bowl includes all food groups and colors. Sweet potato (sweet) supports the spleen. Broccoli (bitter) clears heat. Pickles (sour/salty) aid digestion. Tahini (neutral) harmonizes.

🥘 Dinner: Stir-Fried Tofu with Colorful Vegetables

Ingredients: 200g firm tofu, bell peppers (red, yellow), snap peas, carrots, garlic, ginger, soy sauce, sesame oil

Method: Press and cube tofu, fry until golden. Stir-fry sliced vegetables with garlic and ginger. Add tofu back, season with soy sauce and sesame oil. Serve over rice.

TCM Notes: The colorful vegetables represent the five elements: red (fire/heart), yellow (earth/spleen), green (wood/liver), white (metal/lungs), black/dark (water/kidneys). A truly harmonizing meal.

Alternative Meal Plan

🔄 Alternative Breakfast: Five-Flavor Rice Porridge

Cook rice porridge and top with: goji berries (sweet), a few drops of vinegar (sour), a pinch of green tea powder (bitter), a sprinkle of sea salt (salty), and sliced ginger (pungent). A simple way to practice TCM's five-flavor theory.

🔄 Alternative Lunch: Rainbow Sushi Bowl

Sushi rice topped with sliced avocado, cucumber, carrot, edamame, and smoked salmon or tofu. Drizzle with soy sauce and wasabi. Each color provides different nutrients, making this a naturally balanced meal.

🔄 Alternative Dinner: Five-Spice Chicken with Vegetables

Marinate chicken thighs in Chinese five-spice powder, soy sauce, and honey. Bake at 200°C for 25 minutes. Serve with steamed bok choy and brown rice. Five-spice powder contains all five flavors in one blend.

"Balance is not something you find, it is something you create. In food, as in life, harmony comes from variety." — Traditional Chinese wisdom
