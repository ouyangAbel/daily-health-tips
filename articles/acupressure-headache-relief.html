Acupressure for Headache Relief

📅 June 16, 2026 • Daily Wellness
Advertisement Space (728x90)

Headaches are one of the most common health complaints worldwide, affecting nearly everyone at some point. Before reaching for painkillers, consider a technique that has been used for over 2,000 years in Traditional Chinese Medicine: acupressure.

Acupressure applies firm pressure to specific points on the body to relieve tension, improve circulation, and restore the flow of Qi (vital energy). Unlike acupuncture, it uses no needles—just your fingers—making it a safe, free, and effective remedy you can use anywhere, anytime.

How Acupressure Works for Headaches

In TCM, headaches are often caused by blocked or stagnant Qi flowing through the meridian pathways of the head. Common causes include stress, poor posture, eye strain, and emotional tension. Acupressure works by:

  • Releasing muscle tension around the head, neck, and shoulders
  • Promoting blood flow to the brain
  • Stimulating the release of endorphins (natural painkillers)
  • Restoring smooth Qi flow through the affected meridians

5 Acupressure Points for Headache Relief

1. Yintang (印堂) — Third Eye Point

Location: Directly between your eyebrows, in the slight indentation at the bridge of the nose.

Technique: Use your thumb or index finger to press firmly upward and slightly inward. Hold for 1-2 minutes while breathing deeply. You can also make small circular motions.

Best for: Frontal headaches, sinus headaches, stress, anxiety, insomnia. This is one of the most powerful calming points in the entire body.

2. Taiyang (太阳) — Temple Point

Location: In the depression about one finger-width behind the midpoint between the outer edge of your eyebrow and the outer corner of your eye.

Technique: Use your thumbs to press both temples simultaneously. Apply firm, circular pressure for 1-2 minutes. Close your eyes and breathe deeply.

Best for: Migraines, temporal headaches, eye strain, dizziness. This point directly targets the temporal region where tension headaches often concentrate.

3. Hegu (合谷) — Large Intestine 4

Location: In the webbing between your thumb and index finger, at the highest point of the muscle when thumb and index finger are pressed together.

Technique: Use your opposite thumb to press firmly into this point. Apply deep, steady pressure with small circular motions for 1-2 minutes. You should feel a strong sensation—this means you've found the right spot.

Best for: Frontal headaches, toothaches, sinus pain, overall pain relief. This is known as the "master point" for pain in the entire body. Note: Avoid during pregnancy.

4. Fengchi (风池) — Gallbladder 20

Location: At the base of the skull, in the hollow between the two large neck muscles (trapezius), about two finger-widths out from the center.

Technique: Interlace your fingers behind your head. Use both thumbs to press upward into the hollows at the base of your skull. Hold firm pressure for 2-3 minutes while tilting your head back slightly.

Best for: Tension headaches, neck stiffness, migraines, vertigo. This point is especially effective for headaches caused by poor posture or长时间 looking at screens.

5. Neiguan (内关) — Pericardium 6

Location: On the inner wrist, about three finger-widths from the wrist crease, between the two tendons.

Technique: Use your thumb to press firmly between the two tendons on your inner wrist. Hold for 1-2 minutes on each wrist. Apply steady, deep pressure.

Best for: Headaches with nausea, headaches from emotional stress, car sickness. This point also helps with anxiety and palpitations.

Step-by-Step Headache Relief Routine (5 minutes)

  • Minute 1: Press Yintang (third eye) with your index finger. Breathe slowly and deeply.
  • Minute 2: Press both Taiyang (temple) points with your thumbs in circular motions.
  • Minute 3: Press Hegu (hand) on both hands alternately. Press firmly for 30 seconds each side.
  • Minute 4-5: Press Fengchi (base of skull) with both thumbs. Tilt head back slightly. Hold and breathe.

Tips for Best Results

🔑 Key Principles

  • Pressure should be firm but not painful — you should feel strong sensation, not sharp pain
  • Breathe deeply — slow, deep breaths enhance the effect of each point
  • Be consistent — practice daily for prevention, not just during headaches
  • Stay hydrated — many headaches are caused or worsened by dehydration
  • Combine with warm water — drink a glass of warm water before or after for enhanced effect

When to Seek Medical Help

Acupressure is effective for tension headaches and mild migraines, but see a doctor if you experience:

  • Sudden, severe "thunderclap" headache
  • Headache with fever, stiff neck, confusion, or seizures
  • Headache after a head injury
  • Headaches that are getting progressively worse
  • Headaches that don't respond to any treatment
