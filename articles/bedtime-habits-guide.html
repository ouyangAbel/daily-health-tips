Bedtime Habits: Wind Down for Better Sleep

📅 June 16, 2026 • Fitness & Qigong
Advertisement Space (728x90)

In Traditional Chinese Medicine, the hours between 9-11 PM (the San Jiao period) are considered the ideal time to prepare the body for sleep. The body's energy begins to withdraw inward, and activities that support this transition lead to deeper, more restorative rest.

Modern sleep science confirms what TCM has known for centuries: a consistent wind-down routine signals to the body that it's time to sleep, reducing insomnia and improving sleep quality. Here are traditional Chinese bedtime practices backed by both ancient wisdom and modern research.

10-Minute Bedtime Wind-Down Routine

1. Gentle Stretching (3 minutes)

Before getting into bed, do a series of slow, gentle stretches. Focus on releasing tension in the neck, shoulders, and lower back—areas where the day's stress accumulates. Simple movements like neck rolls, shoulder shrugs, and gentle forward folds help transition the body from activity to rest.

2. Deep Breathing (3 minutes)

Lie on your back with one hand on your chest and one on your belly. Breathe slowly through your nose, allowing your belly to rise first. Exhale through your mouth for twice the duration of your inhale (for example, inhale for 4 counts, exhale for 8). This activates the parasympathetic nervous system, shifting the body into "rest and digest" mode.

3. Abdominal Massage (2 minutes)

Using your right hand, make slow, clockwise circles around your navel. Follow the path of the large intestine: up the right side, across the top, down the left side. This traditional Chinese massage (揉腹) promotes digestion, relieves bloating, and calms the mind before sleep.

4. Foot Massage & Meridian Tapping (2 minutes)

In TCM, the feet contain over 60 acupuncture points connected to every organ in the body. The Yongquan point (Kidney 1), located on the sole of the foot, is especially important for calming the mind and promoting sleep. Massage this point firmly for 1-2 minutes on each foot before sleep.

Foot Soaking: A Traditional Bedtime Ritual

Soaking your feet in warm water (38-42°C) for 15-20 minutes before bed is one of the most popular Chinese bedtime practices. The warmth dilates blood vessels in the feet, drawing blood away from the brain and core, which promotes relaxation and drowsiness. Add ginger or mugwort to the water for extra benefits.

Tips for Better Sleep

  • Stop using electronic devices at least 30 minutes before bed
  • Keep your bedroom cool, dark, and quiet
  • Avoid heavy meals and caffeine after 2 PM
  • Go to bed before 11 PM—this is when the Gallbladder meridian activates for deep rest
  • Sleep on your right side to support digestion, or on your back with a pillow under your knees
  • Keep a consistent sleep schedule, even on weekends
"Sleep is the best meditation. To sleep well is to heal the body and nourish the soul." — Dalai Lama (consistent with TCM sleep philosophy)
