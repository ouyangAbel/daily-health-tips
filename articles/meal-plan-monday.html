Monday - Grounding Day

📅 June 15, 2026 • Week of June 15–21 • Focus: Spleen & Stomach
Advertisement Space (728x90)

Monday's theme is Grounding Day, focusing on spleen and stomach health. In TCM, the spleen governs transformation and transportation of food into energy. Starting the week with grounding, warming foods sets a strong foundation for digestion and sustained energy throughout the week.

Main Meal Plan

🌅 Breakfast: Ginger Congee with Soft-Boiled Egg

Ingredients: 1 cup jasmine rice, 8 cups water, 1 thumb ginger (sliced), 1 soft-boiled egg, scallions, soy sauce

Method: Rinse rice and add to pot with water and ginger slices. Bring to a boil, then simmer on low for 45-60 minutes, stirring occasionally, until thick and creamy. Serve topped with soft-boiled egg, sliced scallions, and a drizzle of soy sauce.

TCM Notes: Congee is the ultimate spleen-nourishing food. Ginger warms the middle jiao and aids digestion. The soft protein from the egg supports Qi without burdening digestion.

🍚 Lunch: Brown Rice with Steamed Vegetables and Tofu

Ingredients: 1 cup brown rice, 200g firm tofu, broccoli, carrots, bok choy, sesame oil, soy sauce

Method: Cook brown rice. Press and cube tofu, pan-fry until golden. Steam broccoli, carrots, and bok choy until tender-crisp. Arrange on rice and drizzle with sesame oil and soy sauce.

TCM Notes: Brown rice is sweet and neutral, supporting the spleen. Tofu is cooling and nourishing. The variety of vegetables provides multiple flavors for organ balance.

🍜 Dinner: Miso Soup with Mushrooms and Seaweed

Ingredients: 4 cups dashi or vegetable broth, 3 tbsp miso paste, 1 cup mixed mushrooms (shiitake, enoki), 2 tbsp dried wakame seaweed, tofu cubes, scallions

Method: Soak wakame in warm water for 5 minutes. Heat broth, add mushrooms, simmer 5 minutes. Remove from heat, dissolve miso paste in a ladle of broth, stir back in. Add wakame, tofu, and scallions.

TCM Notes: Miso is fermented, supporting gut flora. Seaweed is salty and nourishes the kidneys. Mushrooms boost immunity. This light dinner won't burden the digestive system before sleep.

Alternative Meal Plan

If you don't have the ingredients for the main plan, or want to try something different:

🔄 Alternative Breakfast: Oatmeal with Walnuts and Cinnamon

Cook steel-cut oats with water or oat milk. Top with chopped walnuts, a sprinkle of cinnamon, and a drizzle of honey. Walnuts nourish the kidneys (supporting the spleen's partner organ), and cinnamon warms the digestive system.

🔄 Alternative Lunch: Sweet Potato and Lentil Bowl

Roast cubed sweet potato at 200°C for 25 minutes. Cook red lentils until soft. Combine with steamed kale and a tahini-lemon dressing. Sweet potato is one of the best spleen-nourishing foods in TCM—its natural sweetness supports the earth element.

🔄 Alternative Dinner: Chicken and Rice Porridge

Similar to congee but lighter. Simmer shredded chicken breast with rice, ginger, and a pinch of salt until the rice breaks down into a comforting porridge. Add a few goji berries for extra kidney support. This is especially good if you're feeling tired or run-down.

"The spleen and stomach are the roots of heaven and earth. When they are strong, the body flourishes." — Huangdi Neijing (Yellow Emperor's Classic of Internal Medicine)
