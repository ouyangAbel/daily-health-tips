Six Healing Sounds

📅 June 16, 2026 • Qigong Practice
Advertisement Space (728x90)

Chinese Name: 六字诀 (Liù Zì Jué)

Meaning: "Six Character Formula"

The Six Healing Sounds is an ancient Qigong practice that uses six specific vocal sounds to cleanse and heal the internal organs. Each sound vibrates at a frequency that resonates with a particular organ, helping to release stagnant energy, excess heat, and emotional blockages. This practice dates back over 1,500 years and is one of the most accessible Qigong techniques—requiring no movement, just sound and intention.

The Six Sounds

1. Xu (嘘) — Liver 🟢

Element: Wood | Emotion released: Anger | Season: Spring

Sit or stand comfortably. Place your hands on your lower ribs. Inhale deeply through your nose. Exhale slowly through your mouth while making a soft "shhh" sound (like "xu"). Feel the vibration in your liver area (right side under the ribcage). Repeat 6 times. The xu sound helps release frustration, irritability, and pent-up anger.

2. He (呵) — Heart 🔴

Element: Fire | Emotion released: Excitement/Anxiety | Season: Summer

Place your hands on your chest. Inhale deeply. Exhale while making a "haw" sound (like "he" but with mouth open). Feel the vibration in your heart center (middle of chest). Repeat 6 times. The he sound calms an overactive heart, reduces anxiety, and brings inner peace.

3. Hu (呼) — Spleen 🟡

Element: Earth | Emotion released: Worry | Season: Late Summer

Place your hands on your abdomen. Inhale deeply. Exhale while making a "hoo" sound with rounded lips. Feel the vibration in your spleen area (left side under the ribcage). Repeat 6 times. The hu sound eases overthinking, worry, and digestive issues.

4. Si (呬) — Lungs ⚪

Element: Metal | Emotion released: Grief | Season: Autumn

Place your hands on your chest. Inhale deeply. Exhale while making a "sss" sound with teeth slightly together. Feel the vibration across your lungs. Repeat 6 times. The si sound releases grief, sadness, and helps improve respiratory function.

5. Chui (吹) — Kidneys ⚫

Element: Water | Emotion released: Fear | Season: Winter

Place your hands on your lower back (kidney area). Inhale deeply. Exhale while making a "chway" sound with pursed lips. Feel the vibration in your lower back. Repeat 6 times. The chui sound releases fear, insecurity, and supports kidney health.

6. Xi (嘻) — Triple Burner 🌈

Element: All | Emotion released: Stagnation | Season: All

Place your hands on your abdomen. Inhale deeply. Exhale while making a "shee" sound with a smile. Feel the vibration throughout your entire torso. Repeat 6 times. The xi sound balances energy across all three burners (upper, middle, lower) and promotes overall harmony.

How to Practice

  • Practice 10-15 minutes daily, ideally in the morning
  • Practice all six sounds in sequence, or focus on the organ that needs attention
  • The sound should be soft—others shouldn't hear you from across the room
  • Focus your mind on the organ while making the sound—intention amplifies the effect
  • After each sound, take 2-3 normal breaths before moving to the next

Health Benefits

  • Detoxifies internal organs: Each sound vibrates and massages the target organ
  • Reduces emotional stress: Releases stored emotions that cause organ imbalance
  • Improves organ function: Regular practice supports optimal organ health
  • Balances energy: The six sounds together harmonize the entire body
  • Easy to learn: No movement required—just sound and breath
"The six sounds are the voice of the organs. When we speak their language, they respond with health." — Traditional Qigong teaching
